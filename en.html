Travel in Russia

The real country is not in the news, but here.

What haven't we seen there?

According to VCIOM polls, 95% of Russians dream of traveling somewhere, but only 36% plan to spend their vacation in their own country. They say, what haven't we seen here at home? In fact, Russia is a whole universe with the gentle sea of the south, the dense forests of the Sayan Mountains, and the harsh ice of the Putorana Plateau. And you can see all these beauties without millions in your account, a foreign passport, or long flights. For example, Vera Bashmakova — a brave young mother who gathered her three children, put them in her Lada, and drove 20,000 kilometers across her homeland. We have selected and described some interesting places worthy of your vacation.

Curonian Spit

url park-kosa.ru

Here, among forests and sand dunes, you can see two water horizons — the calm Curonian Lagoon on one side and the rippling waves of the Baltic Sea on the other. A unique natural area at the edge of the Russian enclave.

But the Kaliningrad region doesn't end here. For travelers and explorers, nearby is the westernmost point of Russia, the Baltic Spit, and the German heritage of many small seaside towns. The atmosphere of these places excludes any rush, immersing you in the tranquility of nature and the scent of the cool, steely sea.

Kola Peninsula

url National Geographic

Almost the entire peninsula is beyond the Arctic Circle. The Sami tundra, with taiga to the south and the Arctic Ocean to the north, masquerading as the Barents Sea.

Perhaps you’ve seen Zvyagintsev’s films or heard about the Arctic festival in Teriberka. Maybe the word “Khibiny” didn’t get buried under the snow of your school geography lessons. Maybe you’ve never been interested in the superdeep borehole piercing the earth’s crust, and apatite only brings you apathy. But your dream of seeing the northern lights begins with a ticket to Murmansk.

Altai

url Facebook

Altai is one of the most beautiful places in Russia, primarily because of its mountains: if you drive along the ridge, you’ll see slopes covered with pines, mountain rivers, and lakes. And if you open the car windows, you’ll experience the invisible wonder of these places — the mountain air.

The climate in Altai is temperate, so it’s best to visit in summer. This way, you’ll see all the diversity of local flora and fauna. Moose roam the forests, eagles soar above the ridges, and roe deer graze on the plains. The famous manuls (Pallas’s cats) also inhabit the Altai region.

Winter Baikal

url https://vk.com/baikalmile

Baikal is known as the largest lake in the world. Many also know it as the biggest source of fresh water and one of the most beautiful places in Russia.

Of course, all this is true. But Baikal is also the perfect place for skijoring competitions. This is a sport where a skier attaches themselves to a motorcycle, and the tandem tries to reach the highest possible speed on the ice. In March 2019, at the Baikal Mile festival, a world record was set — 197.011 km/h.

Karelia

url http://vodlozero.ru/

Siberia doesn’t end at the Urals, but in Karelia: the Siberian larch, which forms the taiga, doesn’t grow west of Vodlozero. But here it reaches 30 meters — the forests of Karelia’s national parks have never known the axe due to impassable swamps. Some pines here are over half a millennium old. Touch a living being that saw the sun before Ivan the Terrible did. In the virgin forest, you won’t find a trail for a hundred kilometers. And on the rare paths, trees a couple of meters above the ground are marked with bear claws. So everyone knows who’s the boss here.
